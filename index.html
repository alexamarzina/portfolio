Data

For both synthesis projects, I used the Million Song Dataset. Specifically, for synthesis 2, I took the data, made it into lists, then grouped it in a way that I was able to see the amount of the word "love" in song titles for years 1930-2010.


While it's a cool representation, it totally leaves out the individual titles of the songs, which would be cool to look at and compare how each one uses "love," for example.


When this class started, I had no idea what data I wanted to use — or even the importance of raw data, really — so I don't think I particularly wish that I did anything differently. This data was cool and I'm happy with what I was able to produce with it.



Visualization Choice

When I originally couldn't get the line graph to work for synthesis 2, I was thinking of trying to do a bar graph instead. That way, it would still show the amount of "love"s in songs by year. However, I didn't think it would be able to show the progression over time as well as a line graph, so I worked really hard to make it work the way I wanted it to, and I think the visualization was successful in that way.
