Data

For both synthesis projects, I used the Million Song Dataset. Specifically, for synthesis 2, I took the data, made it into lists, then grouped it in a way that I was able to see the amount of the word "love" in song titles for years 1930-2010.


While it's a cool representation, it totally leaves out the individual titles of the songs, which would be cool to look at and compare how each one uses "love," for example.


When this class started, I had no idea what data I wanted to use — or even the importance of raw data, really — so I don't think I particularly wish that I did anything differently. This data was cool and I'm happy with what I was able to produce with it.



Visualization Choice

When I originally couldn't get the line graph to work for synthesis 2, I was thinking of trying to do a bar graph instead. That way, it would still show the amount of "love"s in songs by year. However, I didn't think it would be able to show the progression over time as well as a line graph, so I worked really hard to make it work the way I wanted it to, and I think the visualization was successful in that way.



Design Choice

I got some (well-deserved) flack for my color scheme choice for my skills development assignment. However, I didn't choose these colors for no reason. Since this assignment was all about our learning and personal growth, and was all personal, basically, I thought it would make the most sense to design the site in variations of my favorite color: pink. I think it looks cute, and it's my growth/learning that was documented on this site, so I went with it.


I know it doesn't make sense to make every project in my favorite color, but I think there is a time and a place! So, for the revision of synthesis 2, I used an online tool called "Coolors" to help me choose a cohesive set of colors. I didn't want to pick something that was too neutral and boring, because that's not who I am as a person, plus music data and titles isn't super serious data. I wanted this to be kind of playful. I don't think it's too bright, but you are free to disagree.


If I was presenting this to a music expert, if those exist or need specific qualifications, I'm not sure if I would change the colors or not. I think bright colors put people in a good mood, so I wouldn't want to make it more dull or muted, especially since musicians tend to be more creative and would probably appreciate my "wacky" colors.


Someone like a business man, for example, who probably doesn't know much about the technicalities of music, still probably listens to the radio — at least — on his way home from work and hears the pop music and the love songs and every thing in between and can still probably see the data in the same way despite the site being a little bit bright. That being said, I stand by my color choices!
